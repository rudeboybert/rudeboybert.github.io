Course Description/Goals

This is an advanced undergraduate level probability class where much of the material of this class will be the theoretical backbone of MATH 392: Mathematical Statistics. Students will be exposed to a higher level of mathematical rigor. Goals of this course include
  • Defining probability using the Kolmogorov Axioms
  • Giving a refresher on basic methods of counting, including permutations, combinations, and multinomial coefficients
  • Understanding concepts of probability, including conditional probabilities and Bayes' Theorem
  • Introducing both discrete and continuous random variables and their distribution functions
  • Characterizing random variables via expectation, variance
  • Understanding bivariate random variables: joint, marginal, covariance/correlation
  • Studying two of the most important theorems in probability: the Weak Law of Large Numbers and the Central Limit Theorem
  • Special topics (subject to change): Markov chains/Monte Carlo Methods, random processes, ...
