Course Description/Goals

This is an introductory probability and statistics class intended for primarily for non-math majors. As such, a conceptual understanding of the material will be stressed. Goals of this course include
  • Develop statistical literacy.
  • Develop students' abilities to answer questions using data.
  • To this end, give students experience manipulating and analyzing data using R, the widely used open source statistical software.
  • Introduce the theory underlying statistics, including (but not limited to): probability, distributions, central limit theorem, etc.
  • Expose students to basic statistical tools, including (but not limited to): sampling, visualizations of data, confidence intervals, hypothesis tests, and regression.
  • Tie in the curriculum to current events, thus demonstrating the importance statistics plays in society.
