Course Description/Goals

This is an advanced undergraduate level mathematical statistics class intended for those seeking a more in-depth treatment of the material. The material from MATH 391: Probability serves as the theoretical underpinnings of this class. Students will be exposed to a higher level of mathematical rigor than in a introductory probability and statistics class. Topics in this course include
  • Point estimation: methods of finding estimators and evaluating estimators
  • Interval estimation
  • Hypothesis testing: test construction, evaluation, and examples
  • Asymptotics
  • Linear models: ANOVA, simple linear regression, generalized linear models.
  • Bayesian inference
  • Miscellaneous: Delta method, bootstrap methods, EM Algorithm
